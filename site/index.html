Pakai Niat turns your rambling intentions into an organized life. Type “maghrib at the mosque every day” or “finish report before 5pm, high priority” — the AI catalogs it, your phone remembers it, and streaks keep you honest.

Features

Built for people whose todo apps gave up on them

No forms. No sixteen-field dialogs. Just tell it what you mean — it does the filing.

Capture in plain words

One text box, powered by OpenRouter. Create, rename, reschedule, or complete items in a single sentence — the AI returns structured operations you approve with one tap.

Habits with real stakes

Streaks that increment only once per day (no double-tap cheating), and a Miss button that actually logs the miss — because honest data beats flattering data.

Priorities that infer themselves

“Before 5pm”, “urgent”, “after Friday prayers” — the parser sets priority and deadlines from your wording, not from dropdowns.

Local-first & private

Everything lives in an on-device Isar database. No account, no cloud sync, no analytics. The AI only ever sees the sentence you typed.

Vertical-first design

Most planners are Gantt charts squeezed onto a phone. Pakai Niat is one calm column: Today, Habits, Ideas — thumb-friendly and dark-mode native.

Bring your own brain

Runs on OpenRouter’s free model router — no subscription, no credit card. Add your key to .env and go.

Honest data beats flattering data.

1

Say it

Open Capture and type (or ramble) what you intend to do. “Skin care every 2 days, evening.” That’s the whole form.

2

Mean it

The AI drafts structured operations — type, schedule, priority. You review the preview and hit Apply. Nothing enters your catalog without your nod.

3

Do it

Today shows exactly what’s due. Tap ✓ to build the streak, or ✕ to log an honest miss. Either way, the data stays true.

niat · /nee-aht/

Malay for intention — the thing you mean to do before life gets in the way. “Pakai niat” is what your mother says when “I’ll do it later” stops working: use your intention.
